Guide

How it works

Follow these steps to get the most out of ClipMind.

01
Getting started

Choose your media source

Select one of three input types at the top of the page: YouTube URL, Video file, or Audio file. Paste a YouTube link directly from the address bar — Shorts and Music links work too. For local files, drag them into the drop zone or click "browse files". Supported video formats: MP4, MOV, MKV, AVI, WEBM. Supported audio: MP3, WAV, M4A, OGG, FLAC. Maximum file size is 4 GB for video and 2 GB for audio.

Tip: For best results with local files, use MP4 (H.264) or MP3 format.
02
Transcription

Generate your transcript

Click Transcribe and our AI begins processing your media. For YouTube links, we fetch the audio directly — no download needed on your end. For uploads, the file is sent securely to our servers. Transcription typically takes under 30 seconds for videos up to 30 minutes. Longer content may take up to 2 minutes. You'll see a live progress bar during processing. The result includes timestamps every 20–30 seconds and automatic speaker labels (Speaker A, Speaker B…). Toggle between Words view and Speaker view using the buttons in the transcript header.

Tip: Click any transcript segment to highlight it and jump to that timestamp.
03
Export

Export in any format

Use the Export dropdown in the transcript header to download in six formats. TXT gives you plain text with timestamps. SRT and VTT are subtitle files you can upload directly to YouTube, Vimeo, or any video player. DOCX opens in Microsoft Word or Google Docs. PDF is print-ready. JSON gives you structured data with start/end times for every segment — useful for developers. You can also click Copy all to copy the entire transcript to your clipboard in seconds.

Tip: Use SRT export to add subtitles to your YouTube video instantly.
04
AI Chat

Chat with the AI about your content

Once your transcript is ready, scroll to the Chat section. The AI has read your entire transcript and is ready to answer questions. You can ask for a summary, ask about specific speakers, find timestamps for topics, pull out statistics or quotes, or get a full outline. Click any of the suggestion chips above the input box for one-click questions, or type your own. The AI responds in context — it knows exactly what was said and when. Press Enter to send, or Shift+Enter for a new line in multi-line messages.

Tip: Ask "What timestamp covers topic X?" to navigate long videos quickly.
05
Generate

Generate captioned video or audio narration

In the Generate section you can create two types of output. Video with captions burns the transcript text into the original video as subtitles — choose the caption style (bold, minimal, highlighted word), font size (small/medium/large), and position (bottom, top, middle). Click Generate Video and download the MP4. Audio narration converts the transcript text into AI-generated speech — pick a voice, set the playback speed using the slider (0.5× to 2.0×), and choose MP3 or WAV. Both outputs process in the cloud and are ready in under 60 seconds for most content.

Tip: Use the "Highlighted word" caption style for engaging social media clips.
